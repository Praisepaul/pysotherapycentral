I am an Indian queer-affirmative, trauma-informed RCI licensed clinical psychologist, a member of the British Psychological Society (MBPsS) with 4+ years of experience. I practice psychodynamic therapy. I have experience providing psychotherapy by video conference to people whose location, lifestyle, or circumstance make it difficult to attend in-person meetings.

I work with English and Hindi speaking patients and clients around the world,including people in sensitive and high-profile roles (such as public figures) for whom discretion and privacy are paramount.


My experience providing psychotherapeutic treatment extends to:

In the early stages of my career, I worked as a Psychologist with Apollo Hospital and Max Healthcare. Currently, I work as a consultant Clinical Psychologist, along with my private practice.

About Psychotherapy

Psychotherapy is a means of long-term therapy that can take place between once and five times a week depending on needs. Often things like insomnia, anxiety, phobias, and any out-of-control behaviours, thoughts, or feelings are a communication from the subconscious. Psychotherapy unravels communication so the benefits can be felt. Sessions last a 'therapeutic hour' which is approximately fifty to sixty minutes.

During psychotherapy, psychological interventions can be utilized if appropriate. This will always be discussed and the pros and cons in your individual circumstances looked at before you make a decision about using them. Your therapist will always work in a way that is comfortable to you.
